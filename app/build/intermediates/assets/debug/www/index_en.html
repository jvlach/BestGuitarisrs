1.Jimi Hendrix

Menu
He is generally considered the best rock guitarist ever. That’s right, since his guitar sounds like coming from another planet (even today). Unfortunately, he died very young.
Listen: Hear My Train A Comin'  All Along The Watchtower
Next

2.Chuck Berry

Menu
The first one to play real rock, away from the blues. He started everything and wrote some great songs. Not the easiest person to work with (so they say) but a great guitarist.
Listen: Johnny B. Goode  Maybellene
Previous Next

3.Jeff Beck

Menu
He started earlier that Clapton, but was never a super star. He did a lot of innovative and different things. He kept making great music (not very popular though), when others had no ideas anymore.
Listen: Beck's Bolero  Futher On Up The Road (με Eric Clapton)
Previous Next

4.Eric Clapton

Menu
Great guitarist, especially during his first years. He loves the blues and used them to evolve the rock sound. Also, he is the one that, more or less, introduced us to JJ Cale and Bob Marley.
Listen: While My Guitar Gently Weeps  Crossroads (1968)

Previous Next

5.Rory Gallagher

Menu
Another great guitarist, he never became a star mainly because he didn’t want to. All of his work is great. Unfortunately, he died rather young.
Listen: Moonchild  Philby

Previous Next

6.Jimmy Page

Menu
Don’t need to say a lot for Jimmy Page. He did great things (mainly) with Led Zeppelin.
Listen: Stairway to Heaven  Kashmir

Previous Next

7.Santana

Menu
He brought the latin sound in rock. Very capable guitarist, with a unique, sweet sound.
Listen: Black Magic Woman  Samba Pa Ti

Previous Next

8.Ritchie Blackmore

Menu
Great soloist and one of the first contributors to the neo-classical guitar genre. He was fantastic both with Deep Purple and with Rainbow.
Listen: Highway Star  Lazy

Previous Next

9.Brian May

Menu
He has a unique sound, melodic but also hard when it’s needed. Great work with Queen, did some good stuff as a solo artist too.
Listen: Crazy Little Thing Called Love  Stone Cold Crazy

Previous Next

10.Dave Davies

Menu
Kinks are one of the groups that laid the foundations of hard rock. Dave Davies was one of the main reasons for that.
Listen: You Really Got Me  All Day and All of the Night

Previous Next

11.Frank Zappa

Menu
Incredibly talented and outstanding personality. A great guitarist, among other things.
Listen: Black Napkins  Cosmik Debris

Previous Next

12.Mark Knopfler

Menu
Unique, melodic sound and great technique. The first time one listens to Sultans of Swings is a positive shock.
Listen: Sultans Of Swing  Brothers In Arms

Previous Next

13.Tony Iommi

Menu
The father of heavy metal. Took him some to get recognized as a guitarist, probably due to his hard sound (at least for the 70s).
Listen: Snowblind  Heaven And Hell

Previous Next

14.Dave Gilmour

Menu
Low profile guitarist, some of his work with Pink Floyd is exceptional.
Listen: Comfortably Numb  On An Island

Previous Next

15.Robby Krieger

Menu
Main contributor to The Doors success. He often had to play in a special way, since the group had no bassist.
Listen: Waiting for the sun  Riders On The Storm

Previous Next

16.Muddy Waters

Menu
Blues legend. Main influence for the British Blues scene of the 60s.
Listen: Manish Boy  Rollin' Stone

Previous Next

17.Eddie Van Halen

Menu
Although Van Hallen quickly moved towards FM Rock, Eddie’s talent is shown in their first records. He revolutionized the guitar technique (finger-tapping) and he was copied by many.
Listen: Eruption (live)  Ain't Talkin' 'Bout Love

Previous Next

18.Robert Johnson

Menu
Early blues artist (during the 30s). He died young and was unknown until he was discovered by artists like Eric Clapton.
Listen: Sweet Home Chicago  Rambling On My Mind

Previous Next

19.Mick Ronson

Menu
He was a perfect match for Bowie and they wrote great music together. He was also an active session musician.

Previous Next

Menu

Previous Next

Menu

31.Yngwie Malmsteen

Menu
Underestimated guitarist, he is not usually included in the best guitarists’ lists. Which is strange, since he is one of the technically most advanced guitarists, he has a unique sound and a successful decade (80s).

35.Yannis Spathas

Menu

Menu
